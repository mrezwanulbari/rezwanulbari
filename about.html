About

17+ years building cybersecurity resilience across five sectors and two continents

Professional Journey

Shakil Md. Rezwanul Bari is a cybersecurity architect and framework developer with over 17 years of specialized experience in designing, implementing, and disseminating replicable cybersecurity defense methodologies across critical infrastructure sectors. His career spans financial services, cloud technology, international development, non-profit organizations, and education — giving him a uniquely cross-sector perspective on protecting diverse digital environments.

His professional journey began in academia at the Daffodil Institute of IT in Bangladesh (2004–2006), where he served as a Lecturer in Information Technology and Information Systems. This early commitment to knowledge transfer established a foundation that continues to define his career: the belief that cybersecurity defense is only as strong as the people behind it.

From 2006 to 2011, Shakil served as IT Infrastructure and Security Coordinator at the Mennonite Central Committee, an international non-profit organization, where he managed enterprise IT infrastructure across Bangladesh operations. This role provided formative experience in securing diverse environments with limited resources — a challenge that sharpened his ability to design efficient, scalable security solutions.

A Decade in Banking Cybersecurity

Shakil's ten-year tenure in the banking sector (2011–2023) represents the core of his technical evolution. As Lead Cybersecurity Engineer at NCC Bank PLC (2011–2021), he architected and deployed enterprise-wide security infrastructure protecting over 1,500 endpoints. He developed more than 60 automated security playbooks that reduced Mean Time to Detect and Mean Time to Respond (MTTD/MTTR) by 45%, and built custom integrations using Python, PowerShell, and JavaScript that connected Cortex XSOAR with Splunk, CrowdStrike, and ServiceNow.

At Bank Asia PLC (2021–2023), Shakil led the design and implementation of an end-to-end PCI-DSS v4.0.1 compliance framework that achieved an 80% improvement in the organization's overall security posture. He also developed custom SIEM correlation rules, EDR detections, and YARA/Sigma rules, and conducted advanced digital forensics including memory analysis, disk imaging, and malware reverse engineering.

Current Work in the United States

Since December 2023, Shakil has served as Cybersecurity Architect and AI-Driven Defense Framework Developer at Aspire Tech Services and Solutions Corp. in New York, where he leads cybersecurity defense operations for clients across multiple sectors: Cloud Himalaya (cloud technology), IDRA/World Bank (international development), and BA Express (financial services).

In this role, he has engineered an AI-powered SIEM/SOAR integration methodology using Splunk and Cortex XSOAR that has achieved a 60% reduction in incident response time and a 70% increase in threat mitigation efficiency. He has designed and implemented Zero Trust Architecture per NIST principles and Executive Order 14028, and integrated AI-based deception technologies and User and Entity Behavior Analytics (UEBA) to reduce response times by 50%.

Education & Credentials

Shakil holds a Master of Science (MSc) in Management Information Systems from Daffodil International University, Bangladesh (2006–2008), evaluated as equivalent to a U.S. Master of Science by Morningside Evaluations (June 2023). He also holds a Bachelor (Hons) in Computer and Information Systems from London Metropolitan University, United Kingdom (2000–2004).

He maintains 18 active professional certifications including CISM, CISA, CompTIA CySA+, CompTIA Security+, AWS Certified Solutions Architect – Associate, Azure Cybersecurity Architect Expert (SC-100), Azure Security Engineer Associate (AZ-500), Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH), Splunk Enterprise Security Certified Admin, and ISO 27001:2013 Lead Auditor.

Mission

Shakil's professional mission is to advance U.S. national cybersecurity resilience through three interconnected pillars: developing replicable defense frameworks that can be adopted across industries, applying cybersecurity expertise across diverse critical infrastructure sectors, and building domestic cybersecurity workforce capacity through structured mentorship and knowledge transfer. His work has been recognized by The Daily Observer, which highlighted his role in "fortifying digital defenses" and "transforming cybersecurity infrastructures" across financial, governmental, healthcare, and corporate sectors.
